Conclude

Solar panel specifications tell you the power, current and voltage characteristics for a specific panel at standard test conditions (STC). These characteristics vary because of:

  • Material used
  • Manufacturing process
  • Size of the panel

Even for a specific panel, the actual characteristics will vary based on deviations from STC:

  • Air mass
  • Radiation at that location
  • Temperature

Conclude

Panels are connected in series or parallel to form an array. Series connections will be add the voltages generated by each panel. Parallel connections will add the current, while the voltage stays the same.

Ask and Discuss
  • What are standard test conditions? Is it possible to predict the output from panels when conditions deviate from STC?
  • What factors change the radiation levels?
  • On what all factors do the output from panels vary?
  • If you want the cheapest panel, which PV technology will you choose?
  • If you want the best efficiency, which PV technology will you choose?
  • If you want the panel to last the longest, which PV technology will you choose?
  • By what criteria will you decide whether to connect panels in series or parallel?
