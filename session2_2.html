Introduction

We now know that PV modules produce electricity. But how much electrical power do they provide? Does it vary depending on the load? We can find out with an experiment

 

Play Animation

  • I-V Curve Experiment

 

Conduct Experiment by referring to the lab manual:

  • I-V Curve Lab Manual

 

Conclude

All other things such as temperature and radiation being constant, PV modules deliver different power output at different loads. This can be observed by the I-V curve.

Ask and Discuss
  • Do all 3 parameters - current, voltage and power vary with the load on a PV module?
  • In the case of battery, what happens when the load is changed? Is it same or different from a PV module? If so, why?
  • Is there a load at which we get maximum power from the PV module? Is there a load at which we get zero power? If so, what are those?
